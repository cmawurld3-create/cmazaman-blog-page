Digital Wellness: Taking Control of Your Screen Life

✍ CMA Zaman 📅 2025 ⏱ 5 min read

We live in an era of infinite scroll. Social media feeds designed by behavioral scientists to keep us engaged. Notification systems engineered to trigger dopamine. The average person now touches their phone over 2,600 times a day.

The Hidden Cost of Connectivity

Digital overload is real. Research consistently links excessive screen time to increased anxiety, disrupted sleep, shortened attention spans, and a diminished sense of presence in one's own life. We are more connected than ever, and lonelier than ever.

"We have the most powerful communication tools in history, and somehow we feel less heard than ever before." 👉 Cma Zaman

Reclaiming Your Attention

Attention is the currency of the modern world. Every app, platform, and notification is competing for yours. The first step to digital wellness is recognizing that your attention is valuable, and that you are allowed to protect it.

Practical Steps to Start Today

  • Set phone-free hours: Especially the first and last hour of your day.
  • Turn off non-essential notifications: Let you decide when to check, not the app.
  • Use grayscale mode: Color is a significant driver of compulsive phone use.
  • Create a tech free zones: The dinner table, the bedroom, the bathroom.

Technology as a Tool, Not a Master

Technology is extraordinary. It connects us to knowledge, to loved ones across oceans, to opportunities unimaginable a generation ago. The goal of digital wellness is not to reject technology; it is to use it with intention rather than compulsion.

When you pick up your phone, ask yourself: Am I using this, or is it using me?

That single question can change everything.
